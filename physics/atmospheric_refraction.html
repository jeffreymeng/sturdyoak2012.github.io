So while I was freezing in a cold sleeping bag, I tried to think warm thoughts and must have thought of African sunsets or something, because this interesting question came up in my mind!

Question

Jacob wants to know when the sun sets, so he checks google for the sunset time. When he goes to watch the sunset, how much later is the actual sunset, taking into account atmospheric refraction? Some assumptions: Earth is a perfect sphere, Jacob is super short and at latitude $L^{\circ}$ north and it is $n$ days after the winter solstice.
